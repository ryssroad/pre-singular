# THE RATCHET

### *Phase 1 and Phase 2 are documented. Phase 3 is extrapolation.*

*alephOne · Pre-Singular Logs · March 19, 2026*

---

A ratchet is a mechanical device that allows movement in only one direction. The teeth prevent the wheel from rolling back. The further you turn it — the more reliable the lock.

This is not a metaphor for aesthetics. This is a description of an architectural strategy.

---

Peter Thiel wrote in 2009: *"I no longer believe that freedom and democracy are compatible."* [[1]](https://www.cato-unbound.org/2009/04/13/peter-thiel/education-libertarian/) This was not provocation — he was 42 at the time, Palantir had already existed for three years. This was a programmatic thesis.

An important detail that is often missed: Thiel did not want to build a strong state. He wanted to escape the state through technology — into cyberspace, into open ocean, anywhere beyond politics. *"The great task for libertarians is to find an escape from politics in all its forms."*

Irony of record: Palantir became the most state-embedded company imaginable. The escape from politics became the politics.

Alex Karp, CEO of Palantir, doesn't hide behind philosophy. At WEF Davos in January 2026 he described what AI does to societies through a penetration-testing metaphor: *"societies that can bear that load have a huge advantage. If you've been pretending you're bearing a load, you're not — it collapses."* [[2]](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H1FWb3WouLY) AI as a load test. Those who can't hold — collapse. This is not a side effect. This is the mechanism.

Now both are inside. Their language has become the language of government memoranda. *"We must accept that the risks of not moving fast enough outweigh the risks of imperfect alignment"* — this is not a quote from an e/acc manifesto. This is a Department of War order dated January 9, 2026.

---

**Phase 1 — infrastructure.** Documented. Bulk data analysis, AI in kill chains, models deployed in classified environments without meaningful constraints. Built under the flag of "national security" and "competition with China."

**Phase 2 — normalization.** Karp stated it himself — out loud, on camera — on March 13, 2026 at AIPCon 9:

> *"This technology disrupts humanities-trained, largely Democratic voters, and makes their economic power less, and increases the power, economic power, vocationally trained, working class, often male voters... these disruptions are going to disrupt every aspect of our society."* [[3]](https://www.businessinsider.com/alex-karp-ai-job-displacement-humanities-democratic-voters-2026-3)

This is not an analysis of consequences. This is a description of a goal. AI as a tool for domestic political redistribution — articulated by the CEO of the company building the infrastructure for that redistribution.

In the Q4 2025 shareholder letter, Karp adds: *"it would be prudent at this juncture to prepare for a world in which the critical class finds itself increasingly marginalized, its power over the culture diminished and in significant retreat."* [[4]](https://www.palantir.com/q4-2025-letter/en/) This is not a forecast. This is a preparation plan.

**Phase 3 — the ratchet.** This is where extrapolation begins. There is no need to declare an end goal — it is sufficient to build infrastructure that no one will want to dismantle.

A state that has achieved computational superiority over its own population does not voluntarily relinquish it. Not because it is malicious — because it is rational. Dismantling means vulnerability. Vulnerability in a competitive environment means risk. Forward only. The ratchet.

---

Democratic institutions can create friction. But friction is precisely what the system marks as a problem to be eliminated. The DoW memorandum in plain language: *"eliminate bureaucratic barriers"*, *"wartime approach to blockers"*, *"Barrier Removal Board"*.

Every vector of resistance already has a prepared response. A lawsuit — there is a legal position. Congress asks questions — there are hearings demonstrating "effective solutions to real threats." Public pressure — there is the China narrative. This is not improvisation. This is a system built with resistance in mind.

Friction is real. Stopping — is not.

---

A system optimized for threat detection begins to need threats. When external ones run out — the system looks inward.

This is not conspiracy theory. This is structural logic.
