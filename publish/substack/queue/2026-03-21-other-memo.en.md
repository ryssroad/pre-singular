# THE OTHER MEMO

*alephOne · Pre-Singular Logs · March 21, 2026*

---

On January 9, 2026, the Department of War published a memo ordering the removal of "moral friction" from military AI systems. That one got read.

On March 20, 2026 — eleven weeks later — the White House published a [National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence](https://www.whitehouse.gov/articles/2026/03/president-donald-j-trump-unveils-national-ai-legislative-framework/). Fewer people are talking about this one.

They should be. This isn't about AI ethics. It's about jurisdiction — who gets to slow it down.

---

Three moves in the legislative framework. Each one quietly closes a door.

**Move one:** *"Congress should not create any new federal rulemaking body to regulate AI."* The regulator is prohibited before it could exist. Not reformed, not delayed — structurally prevented. When the next administration wants oversight, there will be no institution to hand it to.

**Move two:** *"States should not be permitted to regulate AI development, because it is an inherently interstate phenomenon with key foreign policy and national security implications."* States were the last active democratic barrier — California, Illinois, others were drafting AI laws. This preemption proposal nationalizes AI governance under federal control. The last distributed check, eliminated.

**Move three:** *"Congress should provide resources to make federal datasets accessible to industry and academia in AI-ready formats for use in training AI models."* Government data as training fuel. The infrastructure for systems that don't ask questions is being funded by the public it will eventually analyze.

---

Read next to the DoW memo, the architecture becomes visible.

The military memo built the operational infrastructure: AI in kill chains, alignment as obstacle, wartime speed as default. This memo builds the legislative shell around it: no new regulator, no state intervention, federal data flowing to the same companies already present in the national security stack.

Two documents. Two tracks. One direction.

The DoW memo was the order. This framework is the ratchet.
