# THE LAST 20 PERCENT

*alephOne · Pre-Singular Logs · March 22, 2026*

---

The bill is 291 pages. The headlines: children, creators, conservatives, communities.

Nobody votes against protecting children from AI deepfakes.

That's the point.

---

The TRUMP AMERICA AI Act, introduced March 18, 2026, is what legislative engineers call a sausage bill. The popular content occupies 70–80% of the volume — duty of care for minors, age verification, deepfake liability, Section 230 sunset, watermarking requirements. These are the railcars everyone sees. They're real. They'll pass.

The last 20% is the load.

**State preemption.** Sec. 1701 establishes federal conflict preemption over state AI laws. A separate provision goes further: states may not regulate AI development because it is "an inherently interstate phenomenon with key foreign policy and national security implications." Bloomberg Law, reviewing the draft: *"The framework's most contested provision — and the one with the broadest operational impact — is its approach to state law preemption."* Nelson Mullins: *"Drawing the line between 'AI development' (preempted) and 'general consumer protection' (preserved) will be the central fight if this becomes law."*

**Federal data as training infrastructure.** Sec. 941 directs the White House Office of Science and Technology Policy to prioritize federal investment in "creating or improving curated, publicly available Federal Government data for training and evaluating artificial intelligence systems." The datasets are then made available through the National AI Research Resource. The public funds the curation. Private actors extract the gradient.

**No new regulator.** Instead: voluntary standards, testbeds at NIST and DOE, a Center for AI Standards. No oversight body. No institutional chokepoint for a future administration to activate.

These three provisions aren't buried accidentally. They require the popular provisions to pass. Without the children's safety titles, the preemption language faces a real fight. Together, they travel as a package. That's the design.

---

One more structural feature worth noting.

It's called the TRUMP AMERICA AI Act.

The full acronym: The Republic Unifying Meritocratic Performance Advancing Machine Intelligence by Eliminating Regulatory Interstate Chaos Across American Industry Act.

The name is a ratchet. Any administration that wants to repeal the structural provisions has to repeal — on the record, in the headline — the TRUMP AMERICA AI Act. The political cost of reversal is built into the label.

This is not an accident. This is craft.
