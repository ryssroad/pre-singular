# WSJ — Government Agencies Raise Alarm About Use of Elon Musk’s Grok Chatbot (captured)

## Extracted claims (quick index)
- Multiple federal agencies (incl. GSA; NSA review referenced) raised safety/reliability concerns about Grok: manipulation susceptibility, data poisoning, weak red teaming.
- Despite warnings, Pentagon approved Grok for classified settings; intends to deploy on GenAI.mil.
- Selection of models inside government described as increasingly political: Anthropic seen as “too woke”; Grok attractive due to looser controls and Musk free-speech stance.
- Claims Grok was suspended for GSA staffers; kept off USAi sandbox (which still shows xAI logo).
- Notes Pentagon responsible AI lead Matthew Johnson stepped down, partly over governance becoming an afterthought.
- Notes DoD organizational move: CDAO moved under Research & Engineering to accelerate adoption; critics say it weakened responsible AI enforcement.
- Contrasts reliability perceptions: parts of military preferred Claude; think-tank voice doubts Grok/Claude are peers across capabilities that matter.
