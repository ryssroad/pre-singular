# WIRED — OpenAI Had Banned Military Use. The Pentagon Tested Its Models Through Microsoft Anyway (captured)

## Extracted claims (quick index)
- In 2023 OpenAI usage policy banned military use, but sources allege DoD experimented with **Azure OpenAI** anyway.
- OpenAI + Microsoft spokespeople say Azure OpenAI is governed by **Microsoft’s terms**, not OpenAI’s policies.
- Reports OpenAI removed blanket military ban by Jan 2024; employees learned via The Intercept.
- Mentions OpenAI–Anduril partnership (Dec 2024) described as narrow/unclassified; contrasts with Anthropic–Palantir for classified.
- Notes Palantir approached OpenAI for “FedStart” in fall 2024; OpenAI declined as too high-risk, but later works with Palantir in other ways.

## Why it matters (editor note)
This is **policy laundering as product architecture**: even if an AI lab claims restrictions, a platform vendor can re-sell the same capability under different terms, and the buyer routes through the least restrictive channel.
