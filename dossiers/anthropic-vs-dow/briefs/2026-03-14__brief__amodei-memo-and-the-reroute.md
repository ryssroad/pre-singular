# Brief: The Amodei Memo and the Reroute (Anthropic vs DoW)

## Thesis
The DoW dispute isn’t a morality play about “killer robots.” It’s a case study in **how power routes around constraints**: contractual red lines, policy bans, and internal safety programs get turned into **interfaces** (theater) or bypassed via **alternate channels** (Microsoft/Azure, integrators, other labs).

## Core materials (primary/anchor)
- The Information (Stephanie Palazzolo) publishes Amodei memo (Mar 4, 2026 1:08pm PST): safety theater, bulk acquired data, HITL policy vs law.
- Anthropic public statements: two exceptions (mass domestic surveillance; fully autonomous weapons), 10 USC 3252 scope.
- WSJ: Altman memo (control framing; cloud-only + FDEs); WSJ Grok alarms (safety reviews overridden; politicization).
- WIRED: OpenAI banned military use; DoD tested via Microsoft anyway (policy laundering).
