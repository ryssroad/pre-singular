# Military AI Split — watchcard

## What it is
A market/tech split where:
- general-purpose frontier labs (e.g., Anthropic) try to constrain certain military uses
- defense-first vendors (Palantir-integrations, Smack-like startups) build domain-specific systems optimized for military decision loops

## Bells
- **Bell #1 (observed):** Palantir demos show LLM chat interfaces supporting intelligence analysis + recommended next steps (WIRED, 2026-03-13)
- **Bell #2 (observed):** Smack claims to train domain-specific models for battlefield planning/decision dominance; $32M round (WIRED, 2026-03-04; Smack site)
- **Bell #3 (to watch):** procurement/fielding evidence (contract awards, program-of-record adoption, training/operational exercises citing these systems)

## Indicators to monitor
- DoD procurement: contract awards, IDIQs, task orders mentioning LLM/mission planning/wargaming/decision dominance
- Integration claims: C2, targeting workflows, logistics planning, sensor fusion
- Quant metrics: wargame outcomes, time-to-plan reductions, operator trust calibration
- Regulatory/legal: “supply chain risk” designations, policy constraints, export controls

## Counter-hypotheses
- Mostly PR + fundraising; real adoption remains limited to analytics/summaries
- Integration stalls due to verification, legal risk, and operational safety constraints
