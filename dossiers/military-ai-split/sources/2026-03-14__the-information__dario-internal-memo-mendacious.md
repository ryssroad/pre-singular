# Dario Amodei internal memo (excerpt) — ‘mendacious’ OpenAI Pentagon messaging

Captured from road drop. Treat as internal memo excerpt; provenance described as paywalled.

## Key claims (index)
- OpenAI/DoD described as offering model access for “all lawful use” plus a “safety layer” (refusals / classifiers / monitoring by FDEs).
- Amodei characterizes this as ~“20% real, 80% safety theater” for military contexts; argues models lack context (HITL, data provenance).
- Notes jailbreak/refusal unreliability and the difficulty of distinguishing context (e.g., domestic vs foreign surveillance) from I/O.
- Claims Palantir offered a similar “safety layer,” framed as something to placate employees / make usage invisible.
- Claims DoD rejected additional safeguards for Anthropic while accepting them for OpenAI.
- Argues “domestic mass surveillance” and “fully autonomous weapons” are not automatically illegal; points to DoD authorities to buy private US citizen data and analyze at scale.
- Claims DoD offered to accept Anthropic’s terms if Anthropic deleted a phrase about “analysis of bulk acquired data.”
- Claims “human in the loop is the law” is false; it is policy that can be changed.

## Raw capture

```
I want to be very clear on the messaging that is coming from OpenAI, and the mendacious nature of it…

…these kinds of approaches… in the context of military applications, maybe 20% real and 80% safety theater…

…refusals aren’t reliable and jailbreaks are common…

…DoW offered to accept our current terms if we deleted a specific phrase about “analysis of bulk acquired data,” which was the single line in the contract that exactly matched the scenario we were most worried about…

…“human in the loop is the law,” but they are incorrect. It is currently Pentagon policy… But that policy can be changed unilaterally…
```
