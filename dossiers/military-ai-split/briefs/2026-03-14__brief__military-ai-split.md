# Brief: Military AI Split (buffer draft set)

## Thesis (1–2 sentences)
Frontier labs can posture about “red lines,” but the market doesn’t stop—it **routes around** them. When a general-purpose model vendor hesitates, specialized defense-first vendors step in and sell “decision dominance” as a product.

## Verified claims (with sources)
1) **Palantir demos + Pentagon records** describe how LLM chat interfaces can support intelligence analysis and recommendations inside defense workflows.  
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-14__wired__palantir-demos-ai-chatbots-war-plans.md`
2) **Smack Technologies** is positioned (and funded) as a defense-first model builder for battlefield planning; claims it will outperform Claude on those tasks.  
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-14__wired__ai-model-military-use-smack-technologies.md`
3) Smack’s own positioning is explicit: domain-specific models for warfare; mission “Deliver Decision Dominance…”  
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-14__smack__about.md`

## Competing hypotheses (to keep us honest)
- H1: Mostly fundraising rhetoric; real deployments remain limited to summarization/analytics, not decision loops.
- H2: DoD adoption is real but remains “human-in-the-loop” enough that the LLM layer is politically tolerable and operationally bounded.

## What we still don’t know
- Procurement reality: contracts, pilots, programs-of-record where LLMs are embedded (not just demo narratives).
- Metrics: wargame performance claims, time-to-plan reductions, failure modes.
