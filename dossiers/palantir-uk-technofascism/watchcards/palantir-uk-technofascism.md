# Palantir UK Technofascism — watchcard

## What it is
A dossier tracking whether UK reliance on Palantir has crossed from ordinary enterprise procurement into a deeper form of strategic dependency: one that combines vendor lock-in, legal sovereignty risk, political alignment, and surveillance capability.

## Current working thesis
The real story may not be "evil software" but institutional exposure: once a politically loaded US defense-data company becomes embedded across health, defense, and critical workflows, the line between procurement convenience and state dependence starts to blur.

## Bells
- **Bell #1 (observed):** Broad UK embedding across NHS, MoD, local/public authorities is being documented in public reporting.
- **Bell #2 (observed / needs hardening):** Nuclear-adjacent and non-tendered strategic contracts suggest deeper entanglement than routine SaaS procurement.
- **Bell #3 (to watch):** Evidence of actual leverage risk — sanctions pressure, unilateral service constraints, intelligence/legal compulsion, or politically contingent access.

## Indicators to monitor
- Crown Commercial Service entries, Contract Finder records, MoD procurement notices
- NHS Federated Data Platform documentation and contract history
- AWE / nuclear supply-chain references involving Palantir or cloud support
- Cloud Act / FISA legal analysis specific to UK public-sector deployments
- Public statements by Palantir, MoD, NHS, or ministers on sovereignty and access
- UK political-network overlap: Reform, advisers, lobbyists, strategic partnership brokers

## Counter-hypotheses
- The exposure is real but mostly administrative/analytics, not operationally coercive
- UK contractual/data controls are stronger than critics imply
- The rhetoric outruns the actual technical and legal threat surface

## Open questions
- What are the exact contract values and dates, by department?
- What parts of the stack are advisory vs mission-critical?
- Are the AWE-related contracts directly operational or generic cloud support?
- What, exactly, can US legal authorities compel from a US vendor in this context?
