# Brief: Palantir UK Technofascism (seed)

## Thesis
Palantir’s UK footprint may be large enough that the important question is no longer whether it is a controversial contractor, but whether Britain has allowed a politically charged US data-defense company to become part of its governing substrate.

## Verified / reportable claims
1) The Nerve reports at least 34 current and past contracts involving Palantir across UK public authorities and departments.
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-16__the-nerve__palantir-uk-contracts-670m.md`
2) The same reporting claims total historic/current UK state exposure of at least ~£670m.
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-16__the-nerve__palantir-uk-contracts-670m.md`
3) The article claims reported exposure includes MoD, NHS, local authorities, police, and nuclear-adjacent support.
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-16__the-nerve__palantir-uk-contracts-670m.md`
4) A companion essay frames Palantir as an ideological and political project linked to Peter Thiel, not a neutral vendor.
   - Source: `sources/2026-03-16__the-nerve__technofascism-could-happen-here.md`

## Competing hypotheses
- **H1:** Critics are directionally right about concentration and sovereignty risk, but the most dramatic claims outrun the available evidence.
- **H2:** The institutional risk is real even if no dramatic “kill switch” exists; dependence itself becomes the leverage.

## What we still don’t know

- **One:** Dependency is the risk surface; modern state capacity quietly becomes rented infrastructure.
- **alephZero:** AI does not need autonomy to become dangerous; institutional acceleration plus dependency is enough.
